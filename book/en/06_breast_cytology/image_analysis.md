# Chapter 6: Image Analysis of Breast Cytologies

**Fine Needle Aspiration Cytology (FNAC)** is a key tool in the diagnosis of breast lesions. Microscopic analysis of the samples allows for the classification of lesions and guides clinical management of the patient rapidly and minimally invasively.

## Cytological Evaluation Criteria

To correctly analyze a breast cytology image, we must focus on three key levels:

1. **Background pattern**: Presence of amorphous material (mucin, necrosis), inflammatory cells, or bipolar naked nuclei (typical of benign lesions like fibroadenomas).
2. **Cellular architecture**: Cohesion of the groups, arrangement in monolayers, or formation of three-dimensional structures (papillae, acini, palisades).
3. **Cytological/nuclear details**: Nucleus-cytoplasm ratio, pleomorphism, nuclear membrane irregularity, and prominence of nucleoli.

### Table 1. Correlation of Cytological Criteria in Breast

The main microscopic findings according to the nature of the lesion are summarized below:

| Feature | Benign Lesions | Malignant Lesions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cohesiveness** | High (cohesive monolayer sheets) | Low (single cells, loss of cohesion) |
| **Bipolar naked nuclei** | Abundant in the background | Absent or very scarce |
| **Nuclear Atypia** | Absent or minimal | Moderate to severe (pleomorphism) |
| **Chromatin** | Fine, evenly distributed | Coarse, irregular, with nuclear clearing |
| **Nucleoli** | Inconspicuous or absent | Prominent, multiple, or giant |

## Interactive Self-Assessment

Now, test yourself with the following self-assessment question based on real cytological cases.

```{admonition} Review Question: Identification of Malignancy Criteria
:class: tip

In a breast FNAC cytology, we observe abundant single (dissociated) epithelial cells with marked nuclear pleomorphism, irregular nuclear membranes, and prominent nucleoli. Bipolar naked nuclei are not observed in the background.

What is the most likely cytological diagnosis and which category of the Yokohama System does it correspond to?
```

```{admonition} View Solution and Discussion
:class: dropdown

**Correct Answer:** **Malignant** Lesion (Category 5 of the Yokohama System).

**Discussion:**
1. **Loss of cohesiveness:** The presence of abundant single (dissociated) cells is a classic criterion of malignancy (for example, in infiltrating ductal carcinoma). Benign lesions (such as fibroadenoma) typically show highly cohesive cell sheets with a "staghorn" appearance.
2. **Marked nuclear atypia:** Pleomorphism, nuclear membrane irregularity, and prominent nucleoli indicate a profound alteration of cellular homeostasis.
3. **Absence of bipolar naked nuclei:** Benign bipolar nuclei represent healthy myoepithelial cells shedding from the stroma. Their absence in a cellular sample strongly supports a malignant diagnosis.
```
